Andrew Valesano, MD, PhD

Resident Physician | Pathologist | Computational Biologist

University of Michigan

I am a physician-scientist at Michigan Medicine training in Anatomic and Clinical Pathology. My research focuses on genomics and data science for improving diagnosis, prognosis, and prediction of therapeutic response in oncology and infectious diseases. Currently, I am working on tumor classification models with methylation profiling, machine learning models to detect precancerous lesions in whole-slide images, and genomics of hematolymphoid neoplasms.

I earned my PhD in 2021 for my work on RNA virus genomics and epidemiology. I developed next-generation sequencing methods to characterize within-patient viral genetic diversity in specimens from clinical trials, large cohort studies, and clinical laboratories. My research provided insights into virus evolution within infected patients and transmission pairs, chronic infections in immunocompromised hosts, and reversion of live-attenuated vaccines. My work included real-time mitigation of local SARS-CoV-2 outbreaks with genomic epidemiology in coordination with public health departments. In addition to work at the bench, I gained expertise in bioinformatics with proficiency in R, Python, and UNIX systems.

You can learn more about my work on my Google Scholar page and find analysis code published on GitHub.
