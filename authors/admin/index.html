Andrew Valesano, MD, PhD

physician-scientist | pathologist | computational biologist | virologist

University of Michigan

Hi, I’m Andrew.

I am a physician-scientist focused on molecular diagnostics and data science in healthcare. Currently, I am a resident physician in Anatomic and Clinical Pathology at Michigan Medicine.

I am excited about leveraging my experience to advance precision medicine and data science in pathology. My goal is to integrate molecular testing with artificial intelligence for cancer diagnosis, prognostic tools, and biomarker development.

I received a PhD in 2021 for my work on RNA virus genomics and epidemiology. I used deep sequencing of samples from clinical cohorts to understand within-patient virus evolution. I also helped build a SARS-CoV-2 genomic surveillance program for real-time outbreak mitigation. In addition to work at the bench, I developed expertise in bioinformatics and computational biology with proficiency in R, Python, UNIX, and high-performance computing.

  • Genomics and computational biology
  • Data science, AI/ML, and informatics in medicine
